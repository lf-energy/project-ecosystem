# Glossary

Terms and acronyms used across LF Energy project overviews. Target audience: utility engineers with familiarity in digital systems.

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-13

## Project Name Aliases

- **Dynawo / Dynaωo** — The project's official name uses the Greek omega symbol (ω), but is commonly spelled "dynawo" in plain ASCII. Both refer to the same project. Directory: `dynaωo/`, overview: `dynaωo.md`.

## Industry Terms & Acronyms

- **AEU** — Avoided Energy Use. The difference between a counterfactual baseline (predicted consumption without intervention) and actual post-intervention consumption. The primary output metric of measurement and verification (M&V) for demand-side programs.
- **AAR** — Ambient-Adjusted Rating. A transmission line thermal rating calculated based on real-time or forecasted ambient weather conditions rather than conservative seasonal assumptions. Required by FERC Order 881 for FERC-jurisdictional transmission facilities. AARs can unlock additional transfer capacity compared to static seasonal ratings.
- **AMI** — Advanced Metering Infrastructure. The system of smart meters, communication networks, and head-end software that enables two-way communication between utilities and customer meters. Next-generation AMI (sometimes called "AMI 2.0") extends beyond basic metering and billing to support edge applications such as grid analytics, DER orchestration, and waveform capture.
- **AGR** — Aggregator. Entity that manages portfolios of prosumers and flexible assets (e.g., heat pumps, EV chargers, batteries, industrial loads), maximizing the value of their flexibility by trading it with grid operators or balance responsible parties.
- **ADMS** — Advanced Distribution Management System. Software platform that combines SCADA, DMS, and OMS functions for distribution grid operations.
- **CalTRACK** — Methodology specification for calculating metered energy savings, developed through a California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) working group starting in 2015. Defines standardized approaches for billing, daily, and hourly baseline models used in pay-for-performance energy efficiency programs. Implemented by OpenDSM.
- **CIS** — Customer Information System. Core utility back-office platform that manages customer accounts, service agreements, rate plan assignments, billing, and payment processing. The system of record for who a utility's customers are, what services they receive, and how they are charged. URPX standardizes the rate plan data object that CIS platforms manage.
- **CIM** — Common Information Model. A family of IEC standards (IEC 61970/61968/62325) that define a common data model for representing power system objects (substations, lines, generators, etc.) at the enterprise level. Used for grid model exchange, planning, and operations. See also CIM-CGMES.
- **CIM-CGMES** — Common Grid Model Exchange Standard. Profile of CIM (IEC 61970/61968/62325) for exchanging power system network models between utilities and coordination centers.
- **CPO** — Charge Point Operator. Entity that operates and maintains a network of EV charging stations.
- **CPMS** — Charging Point Management System. See CSMS.
- **CSMS** — Charging Station Management System. Cloud software that remotely manages a network of EV chargers — handling session tracking, authorization, availability monitoring, and firmware updates. Communicates with chargers via OCPP. "CSMS" is the term introduced by OCPP 2.0.1; the older term "Central System" (and the informal "CPMS") refer to the same functional role.
- **ASHRAE Guideline 14** — ASHRAE measurement standard for evaluating energy savings from building retrofits and efficiency programs. Defines fractional savings uncertainty (FSU) calculations used in M&V. Referenced by OpenDSM for uncertainty quantification.
- **Battery cycler** — Laboratory test equipment that charges and discharges battery cells under controlled conditions, recording voltage, current, and temperature over time. Major manufacturers include Maccor, Arbin, and Neware. The raw data output format is proprietary to each manufacturer — a key problem the Battery Data Alliance addresses.
- **BattINFO** — A domain ontology for batteries and electrochemistry, developed through the Battery2030+ and BIG-MAP European research initiatives. Provides machine-readable vocabulary and relationships for battery concepts (cell, electrode, electrolyte, etc.). The Battery Data Alliance's BDF ontology extends BattINFO.
- **BMS** — Battery Management System. Electronic system that monitors and protects battery cells during charging and discharging, tracking state of charge, temperature, and cell balance. Essential component of any battery energy storage system, from residential batteries to grid-scale BESS.
- **BDF** — Battery Data Format. An open standard defined by the Battery Data Alliance for structuring battery cycling test data. Specifies a fixed table schema with required columns (test time, voltage, current), recommended columns, and optional columns, using IUPAC/SI notation. Designed to make battery datasets interoperable across laboratories, cycler vendors, and analytical tools.
- **BEMS** — Building Energy Management System. Software platform that monitors and controls energy consumption within commercial or institutional buildings, coordinating HVAC, lighting, and other building systems to optimize energy use, comfort, and cost.
- **BESS** — Battery Energy Storage System. A system that stores electrical energy in batteries for later use, typically comprising battery modules, inverters, and a battery management system. Used for grid services (frequency regulation, peak shaving), renewable energy integration, and behind-the-meter optimization.
- **BRP** — Balance Responsible Party. Entity financially responsible for maintaining balance between electricity supply and demand within its portfolio. Imbalances result in settlement charges from the TSO.
- **DER** — Distributed Energy Resource. Small-scale generation or storage connected to the distribution grid (solar PV, batteries, etc.).
- **DERMS** — Distributed Energy Resource Management System. Software platform for monitoring, managing, and coordinating distributed energy resources (solar, batteries, EVs, flexible loads) across a distribution grid, including dispatching flexibility and interfacing with market platforms.
- **DMS** — Distribution Management System. Software platform for monitoring, controlling, and optimizing distribution grid operations, typically including fault detection, switching, and voltage management.
- **DLMS/COSEM (IEC 62056)** — Device Language Message Specification / Companion Specification for Energy Metering. The dominant smart meter communication standard in Europe, defining data models and transport protocols for reading and managing electricity and gas meters.
- **DSM** — Demand-Side Management. Programs and strategies that utilities use to influence customer energy consumption patterns, including energy efficiency, demand response, electrification, and load shifting. See also M&V.
- **DSO** — Distribution System Operator. Entity responsible for operating and maintaining a distribution grid.
- **EEBus** — Open communication standard for energy management in buildings and homes. Enables smart devices (EV chargers, heat pumps, batteries) to coordinate energy usage with a home energy management system.
- **EMT** — Electromagnetic Transient. Simulation approach that models the full electromagnetic behavior of power system components, capturing fast dynamics (microseconds to milliseconds). Computationally intensive but highly detailed; quasi-EMT approaches offer a middle ground between EMT and phasor-based simulation.
- **EAC** — Energy Attribute Certificate. A tradable instrument that certifies the generation of a unit of energy (typically 1 MWh) from a specific source, used to track and verify renewable energy claims. Regional variants include RECs (Renewable Energy Certificates) in North America and GOs (Guarantees of Origin) in Europe.
- **EMS** — Energy Management System. In transmission grid operations, the software platform for monitoring and controlling the bulk power grid (e.g., SCADA/EMS at a control center). Also used more broadly for building- or site-level energy optimization systems that schedule behind-the-meter assets — a different product category with different buyers and use cases.
- **FERC Order 881** — Federal Energy Regulatory Commission order requiring the use of ambient-adjusted ratings (AARs) for transmission lines instead of static seasonal ratings. Mandates that Transmission Owners produce 240-hour-ahead rating forecasts hourly and exchange them with ISOs/RTOs. Also requires AARs in day-ahead and real-time market models. The TROLIE specification was created to standardize the data exchange required by this order.
- **EVSE** — Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment. The physical charging station hardware that delivers electricity to an EV, including the connector, cable, and control electronics.
- **Green Button** — Consumer-facing standard for accessing energy usage data, based on NAESB REQ.21 ESPI (Energy Services Provider Interface). Defines XML/Atom-based data formats for interval usage data. Green Button Download My Data (DMD) enables customers to export their data; Green Button Connect My Data (CMD) enables authorized third-party access via APIs. Has regulatory mandates in several US states. URPX provides formal semantic mappings to Green Button charge categories.
- **GOPACS** — Grid Operators Platform for Congestion Solutions. Dutch national platform operated jointly by grid operators (DSOs and TSO) for market-based congestion management, matching flexibility needs with aggregator capacity.
- **GOOSE** — Generic Object Oriented Substation Event. An IEC 61850 communication protocol for fast, multicast peer-to-peer messaging between substation devices, used for protection tripping and interlocking signals.
- **EDSN** — Energie Data Services Nederland. Dutch energy data services organization that manages shared data infrastructure for the Dutch energy sector.
- **ENTSO-E** — European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity. Coordinates TSOs across Europe and defines frameworks for cross-border grid operations.
- **Industrial Data Historian** — Software platform that collects, stores, and retrieves high-volume time-series data from industrial process control systems (SCADA, DCS, PLCs) at high resolution. Used extensively in power generation and process industries for operational monitoring, trend analysis, and regulatory compliance. Traditional historians (e.g., OSIsoft PI, Honeywell PHD, AspenTech IP.21) are typically deployed on-premises at individual plants or sites.
- **ISO/RTO** — Independent System Operator / Regional Transmission Organization. Entity responsible for operating the bulk power grid and wholesale electricity markets within a defined region in North America. ISOs and RTOs coordinate transmission operations, dispatch generation, and ensure grid reliability. Examples include MISO, PJM, ISO New England, SPP, CAISO, ERCOT, and NYISO.
- **ICCP** — Inter-Control Center Communications Protocol (also IEC 60870-6/TASE.2). Protocol for real-time data exchange between control centers, primarily used for SCADA analog point exchange between ISOs/RTOs and Transmission Owners. Designed for small numbers of analog telemetry points; not suited for the high-volume forecast data exchange required by FERC Order 881.
- **HEMS** — Home Energy Management System. Software or device that monitors and optimizes energy consumption within a residential setting, coordinating smart appliances, solar PV, batteries, EV chargers, and heat pumps to minimize cost or maximize self-consumption.
- **HMI** — Human-Machine Interface. Operator display and control interface for monitoring and interacting with substation or grid equipment.
- **HVDC** — High-Voltage Direct Current. Transmission technology used for long-distance power transfer and interconnecting asynchronous grids.
- **IEC 61851** — International standard for electric vehicle conductive charging systems. Defines physical signaling, safety requirements, and communication between EVs and charging stations (e.g., the PWM pilot signal for AC charging).
- **IEEE C37.118** — IEEE standard for synchrophasor measurements and data transfer. Defines the format for PMU data frames (voltage/current phasors, frequency, rate of change of frequency) and the communication protocol between PMUs, PDCs, and applications. The primary data interface for WAMS.
- **IEC 61850** — International standard for substation automation, defining data models, communication protocols, and configuration language (SCL) for substation devices.
- **IED** — Intelligent Electronic Device. A microprocessor-based device in a substation that performs protection, monitoring, or control functions (e.g., digital protection relays).
- **ISO 15118** — International standard for vehicle-to-grid communication interface. Defines high-level communication between EVs and charging stations, enabling features like Plug & Charge (automated certificate-based authentication) and bidirectional power transfer (V2G).
- **IEEE 1588 (PTP)** — Precision Time Protocol. Standard for sub-microsecond time synchronization across networked devices, critical for time-stamped measurements in substations.
- **NEVI** — National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure. U.S. federal program established by the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (2021) that provides funding to states for building out a national EV charging network along highway corridors. NEVI-funded stations must meet specific technical requirements including OCPP 2.0.1 support.
- **N-k contingency analysis** — Security analysis that evaluates grid stability when k elements (lines, transformers, generators) are simultaneously lost.
- **PAC** — Protection, Automation, and Control. The functional domain covering substation protection relays, automation systems, and control equipment.
- **PDC** — Phasor Data Concentrator. A device or software application that collects, time-aligns, and forwards synchrophasor data from multiple PMUs. Serves as the aggregation point between field PMUs and WAMS applications.
- **PMU** — Phasor Measurement Unit. A device installed at substations that measures voltage and current phasors (magnitude and phase angle) with GPS-synchronized timestamps, typically at 10–60 samples per second. Provides the high-resolution, time-synchronized measurements that enable wide-area monitoring of grid dynamics.
- **PSC** — Public Service Commission. State-level regulatory body that oversees utility rates, service quality, and infrastructure investments. PSC filings (tariff statements) are the formal regulatory documents that define approved utility rate plans. Also referred to as Public Utilities Commission (PUC) in some states.
- **PST** — Phase Shifting Transformer. A transformer that controls active power flow on transmission lines by adjusting voltage phase angle.
- **PTDF / PSDF** — Power Transfer Distribution Factor / Phase Shifter Distribution Factor. Sensitivity factors used to assess how power injections or phase shifter adjustments affect flows on transmission lines.
- **OPC UA** — Open Platform Communications Unified Architecture. Industrial communication standard for secure, platform-independent data exchange between devices, control systems, and enterprise applications. Widely used in manufacturing, process industries, and power generation for reading sensor and process data from PLCs, DCS, and other industrial equipment. RTDIP includes transformers that parse OPC UA JSON data formats (e.g., from OPC Publisher) but does not implement the OPC UA protocol itself.
- **OCPP** — Open Charge Point Protocol. Communication protocol between EV charging stations and central management systems (CPMS). Versions include OCPP 1.6 (widely deployed), OCPP 2.0.1, and OCPP 2.1. Standardized as IEC 63584.
- **OCA** — Open Charge Alliance. Industry consortium that develops and maintains the OCPP and OSCP standards for EV charging. Also operates certification programs for OCPP-compliant products.
- **OCPI** — Open Charge Point Interface. Communication protocol for EV charging roaming, enabling data exchange between CPOs and eMSPs (e-Mobility Service Providers) for cross-network charging access, tariff information, and session data. Current version is OCPI 2.2.1.
- **OMS** — Outage Management System. Software platform for tracking, managing, and restoring power outages across a distribution network.
- **OpenADR** — Open Automated Demand Response. Communication standard for exchanging demand response signals between utilities/grid operators and end devices or aggregators. Uses a VTN/VEN architecture. Managed by the OpenADR Alliance; current versions are 2.0b (2015) and 3.0 (2024). Implemented by the OpenLEADR project.
- **RCC** — Regional Coordination Center. Entity that coordinates grid operations across multiple TSOs in a region (e.g., BalticRCC, Coreso).
- **SAREF** — Smart Applications REFerence ontology. An ETSI standard (TS 103 264) that provides a shared vocabulary and data model for IoT and smart energy applications, enabling devices and platforms from different vendors to describe data in a common way. Used by the Semantic Energy Framework as the default ontology for semantic interoperability.
- **SCADA** — Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition. System for real-time monitoring and control of industrial processes, used in grid operations to collect measurements and issue commands to field equipment.
- **SunSpec** — Industry alliance and communication standard for solar PV, energy storage, and other distributed energy resources. Defines data models and communication protocols (typically over Modbus TCP or REST) that enable monitoring and control of DER equipment from multiple vendors through a common interface.
- **SCD** — System Configuration Description. An IEC 61850-6 SCL file type that contains the complete substation automation system configuration: single-line diagram, logical nodes, communication network, and all IED configurations.
- **SCL** — Substation Configuration Language. XML-based configuration language defined by IEC 61850-6 for describing substation automation systems. Encompasses multiple file types including SSD, SCD, ICD, IID, CID, SED, ISD, and STD.
- **SMV** — Sampled Measured Values. An IEC 61850 communication protocol for streaming digitized analog measurements (voltage and current waveforms) from instrument transformers to protection and metering devices over Ethernet.
- **SSD** — System Specification Description. An IEC 61850-6 SCL file type that describes the substation automation system based on the single-line diagram and logical node representation, before IED-specific configuration is added.
- **TO** — Transmission Owner. Entity that owns transmission facilities on the bulk electric system. In North America, TOs are responsible for maintaining and rating their facilities and submitting those ratings to the ISO/RTO. Distinct from TSO (the European term for the entity that both owns and operates the transmission grid).
- **TOU** — Time of Use. Rate structure where electricity prices vary by time period (on-peak, off-peak, mid-peak), incentivizing customers to shift consumption to lower-cost hours. A key rate plan type that URPX represents.
- **TSO** — Transmission System Operator. Entity responsible for operating and maintaining a transmission grid.
- **USEF** — Universal Smart Energy Framework. Framework for smart energy systems that defines market roles, interactions, and coordination mechanisms for flexibility trading. The USEF Flex Trading Protocol (UFTP) is the subset focused on flexibility exchange between aggregators and grid operators, implemented by the Shapeshifter project.
- **UFTP** — USEF Flex Trading Protocol. See USEF.
- **UCTE-DEF** — Legacy European grid exchange format, predecessor to CIM-CGMES. Still used in some contexts for backward compatibility.
- **WECC** — Western Electricity Coordinating Council. Regional entity responsible for bulk electric system reliability in western North America. Also defines standard dynamic simulation models for wind turbines, PV, and other generation resources that are widely used internationally.
- **WAMS** — Wide Area Monitoring System. A system that uses time-synchronized PMU measurements from across a transmission grid to provide real-time visibility into grid dynamics (oscillations, voltage stability, frequency deviations) at millisecond resolution, complementing traditional SCADA monitoring which operates at second-to-minute timescales.
- **WAMPAC** — Wide Area Monitoring, Protection, and Control. Extension of WAMS that adds automated protection actions (e.g., islanding detection, system integrity protection schemes) and control functions (e.g., corrective switching, generation redispatch) based on wide-area PMU measurements.
- **VEN** — Virtual End Node. In the OpenADR architecture, the client that receives demand response signals from a VTN and reports back on its response. Typically runs on or represents a device, aggregator, or building management system.
- **VTN** — Virtual Top Node. In the OpenADR architecture, the server operated by a utility or aggregator that publishes demand response programs, events, and price signals to VENs.
- **VPP** — Virtual Power Plant. An aggregation of distributed energy resources (batteries, EV chargers, heat pumps, industrial loads) coordinated to operate as a single dispatchable resource for grid services or market participation. The aggregated portfolio is managed by software (typically a DERMS or aggregator platform) that optimizes dispatch across the individual assets.
- **Value stacking** — The practice of a single energy asset (typically BESS) participating in multiple revenue streams simultaneously — e.g., energy arbitrage, ancillary services, and capacity markets — to maximize total returns. Requires optimization software to allocate the asset's capacity across markets while respecting physical constraints.
- **V2G** — Vehicle-to-Grid. Technology enabling bidirectional power flow between an EV battery and the electricity grid, allowing EVs to serve as distributed energy storage.
- **V2H** — Vehicle-to-Home. Technology enabling an EV battery to discharge power back to a home, providing backup power or enabling self-consumption of solar energy.
- **vIED** — Virtual Intelligent Electronic Device. An IED running as software (virtual machine) on a virtualization platform rather than on dedicated hardware.

## Software & Infrastructure Terms

- **ANSSI BP-028** — French national cybersecurity agency (ANSSI) hardening guide for GNU/Linux systems. Used as a compliance benchmark for SEAPATH security validation.
- **Ansible** — Open source IT automation tool for configuration management and deployment. Used by SEAPATH for infrastructure-as-code provisioning.
- **Ceph** — Distributed storage system that replicates data across cluster nodes. Used by SEAPATH to ensure VM disk images survive hardware failures.
- **Corosync / Pacemaker** — Linux high-availability clustering stack. Corosync handles cluster communication; Pacemaker manages resource failover. Used by SEAPATH for automatic VM migration when a server fails.
- **KVM** — Kernel-based Virtual Machine. Linux hypervisor that enables running virtual machines with near-native performance.
- **PREEMPT_RT** — Real-time patch for the Linux kernel that provides deterministic scheduling guarantees, enabling time-critical applications to meet strict latency requirements.
- **PSS/E** — Power System Simulator for Engineering (Siemens). Widely used commercial tool for transmission system analysis; PowSyBl supports its data format.
- **Foundation model** — A large AI model pre-trained on broad data that can be fine-tuned for specific downstream tasks. In the power systems context, GridFM applies this approach using graph neural networks trained on power flow data, which can then be adapted for tasks like contingency analysis or optimal power flow approximation.
- **GNN** — Graph Neural Network. A type of neural network designed to operate on graph-structured data, where information is represented as nodes and edges. In power systems, buses map to nodes and branches to edges, making GNNs a natural fit for learning from grid topology and operating data.
- **LCT** — Low Carbon Technology. Umbrella term for household-level technologies that reduce carbon emissions from energy use, including heat pumps, electric vehicles, solar PV, and home batteries. Used in synthetic smart meter data contexts to describe conditioning attributes for load profile generation.
- **L2RPN** — Learning to Run a Power Network. International competition series using Grid2Op as the platform for benchmarking automated control strategies for power grid operations. Hosted at machine learning venues including NeurIPS.
- **MATPOWER** — Open source MATLAB-based power system simulation package widely used in research. Provides standard test case files (.m format) representing power networks of various sizes. GridFM uses MATPOWER-format networks (including the PGLib benchmark library) as input for generating training datasets.
- **Microgrid** — A localized energy system that can operate independently from the main grid (islanded) or connected to it. Typically includes local generation (e.g., solar PV), energy storage (batteries), and loads, with a controller managing power flows within the system. DC microgrids use direct current for internal power distribution; AC microgrids use alternating current.
- **Matter** — Open smart home connectivity standard (formerly Project CHIP) maintained by the Connectivity Standards Alliance. Provides a unified application-layer protocol for smart home devices (lighting, HVAC, EV chargers, batteries) across multiple transport protocols (Wi-Fi, Thread, Ethernet). Increasingly relevant for grid edge interoperability as utilities seek to interact with behind-the-meter devices.
- **M&V** — Measurement and Verification. The process of quantifying energy savings from demand-side programs by comparing actual post-intervention consumption to a modeled baseline of what consumption would have been without the intervention. See also AEU, CalTRACK, ASHRAE Guideline 14.
- **Modelica** — Open, equation-based modeling language used across industrial sectors (automotive, building, power systems). Models are written as readable mathematical equations rather than procedural code, enabling transparent sharing and review of modeling assumptions. Used by Dynaωo for power system component models.
- **VT-x / VT-d** — Intel hardware virtualization extensions. VT-x enables CPU virtualization; VT-d enables direct device assignment to virtual machines. Required for SEAPATH's real-time VM performance.
